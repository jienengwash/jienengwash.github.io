How to Choose an Industrial Steam Cleaner: 6 Key Parameters

By Guangdong Jieneng 2026-07-16
← Back to Blog

Buying an industrial steam cleaner can be tricky — too little power and parts don't get clean; too much power and you've wasted budget. This guide uses 6 core parameters to help you select precisely.

1. Steam Temperature: 140°C vs 210°C Makes a Huge Difference

Steam temperature directly determines cleaning power. At 140°C, steam dissolves ordinary oils. At 170°C, it softens carbon deposits. At 210°C, it breaks down sintered oil films and resins. Selection guide: Precision parts cleaning — 140-170°C is sufficient. Engine components and molds — 170-210°C. Heavy oil contamination — 210°C minimum. Jieneng JNX-18 and above models all reach 170-210°C, covering 90% of industrial cleaning scenarios.

2. Steam Pressure: Higher Isn't Always Better

Pressure selection depends on workpiece material and contamination type. 4-6 bar for electronic components and precision parts (low-pressure steam prevents deformation). 10-15 bar for automotive parts and hardware (standard industrial cleaning). 15-25 bar for heavy oil, rust, and weld spatter (stubborn contamination). Jieneng's full range features adjustable pressure — one machine handles different workpieces by simply adjusting pressure, eliminating redundant purchases.

3. Steam Flow Rate: The Key to Cleaning Speed

Higher flow rate means more steam per unit time and faster cleaning. The JNX-6 delivers 5 L/h for intermittent small-batch cleaning. The JNX-18 delivers 36-54 L/h for mid-size workshop continuous operation. The JNX-48 reaches 96-144 L/h for production-line-level continuous cleaning. Selection formula: daily cleaning volume ÷ per-unit cleaning time = required steam flow rate, with 20% margin.

4. Tank Capacity: Affects Continuous Operating Time

Small tanks require frequent refilling, disrupting cleaning rhythm. An 18L tank suits mid-range models with 30-45 minutes of continuous operation. A 60L tank suits high-power models with 1-2 hours runtime. For 24/7 production lines, choose models with external water supply connectivity — like the Jieneng JNX-G36/G40, which connects directly to factory water systems without manual refilling.

5. Power Supply Requirements

Models under 2 kW support 220V single-phase power for small workshops or labs. Models above 6 kW require 380V three-phase power, available in most factory workshops. Models above 48 kW need verification of transformer capacity. Always confirm workshop power specifications before purchase. Jieneng supports 220V/380V options with custom voltage adaptation available.

6. Safety Features: Explosion-Proof and Protection Rating

Standard workshops need IP54 protection rating. Chemical, oil & gas, and mining environments require explosion-proof models with ATEX or EAC certification. The Jieneng JNX-EX20 and JNX-G36 carry both EU ATEX and Russian EAC explosion-proof certifications — the only compliant choice for hazardous area cleaning. Safety features also include automatic water-shutoff, over-temperature cutoff, and over-pressure relief, all standard across Jieneng's range.

Summary

Don't focus only on price and power. Temperature, pressure, flow rate, tank capacity, power supply, and safety — all 6 parameters matter.
