How to Choose an Industrial Cleaning Equipment Manufacturer: 5 Key Differences

By Guangdong Jieneng 2026-07-16
← Back to Blog

The biggest fear when buying industrial cleaning equipment is encountering a trading company masquerading as a manufacturer — inflated prices, no after-sales support, and parts supply disruptions. This article teaches 5 methods to identify genuine source manufacturers.

1. R&D and Production Capability

Source manufacturers have their own R&D teams and production workshops, providing patent certificates and technical drawings. Trading companies only have offices and showrooms. Guangdong Jieneng holds multiple steam cleaning technology patents with a factory in Shantou, Guangdong — welcome to visit. Before purchasing, request: business license (scope must include "manufacturing"), patent certificates, and production workshop videos or on-site visits.

2. Customization: The Litmus Test

Traders can only sell standard products. For non-standard requirements, they subcontract to upstream manufacturers — adding a layer of markup and communication inefficiency. Source manufacturers design custom solutions directly: fixture design, cleaning program development, production line layout. Jieneng supports full-dimension custom manufacturing from single-machine modifications to turnkey automated cleaning lines.

3. Certifications: Cannot Be Faked

Source manufacturers invest heavily in certifications requiring factory audits and product testing — impossible for traders to obtain. Key certifications:

  • National High-Tech Enterprise (certified by Ministry of Science & Technology)
  • Specialized & Sophisticated Enterprise (certified by MIIT)
  • ISO 9001/14001/45001 triple certification
  • EU ATEX explosion-proof certification (explosion-proof models only)
  • Third-party整机 test reports

Jieneng holds all of the above, with explosion-proof models carrying dual ATEX + EAC certification.

2. After-Sales Service

Traders depend on upstream manufacturers — 3-7 day response times, 2-4 week parts supply. Source manufacturers have in-house technical teams and parts inventory: 48-hour on-site service, 24-hour parts shipping. Jieneng provides nationwide service + 3-year warranty + lifetime support with critical parts in stock.

3. Price Transparency

Traders add 15-25% markup with opaque pricing (tax, shipping, installation often excluded). Source manufacturers provide clear quotes including tax, shipping, and installation. But beware: prices that are too low may indicate small workshops substituting carbon steel for stainless or domestic pumps for imported ones. Always specify materials and component brands in the purchase contract.

Procurement Checklist

  1. Request a video tour of the production workshop
  2. Ask for 3+ same-industry customer references with contact info
  3. Bring workpieces for on-site testing
  4. Specify material brands, component models, warranty terms, and response times in the contract
  5. Refuse suppliers who won't quote until a deposit is paid
