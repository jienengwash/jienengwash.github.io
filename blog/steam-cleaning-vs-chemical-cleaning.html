Steam Cleaning vs Chemical Cleaning: 6-Dimension Comparison

By Guangdong Jieneng 2026-07-16
← Back to Blog

Steam or chemical solvents for industrial cleaning? This question troubles many procurement managers. This article provides an objective comparison across 6 dimensions.

1. Cleaning Power

Chemical cleaning relies on solvents to dissolve contaminants — effective for oils but limited on carbon deposits and sintered soils, requiring different solvents for different contaminants. Steam cleaning uses high-temperature, high-pressure physical force: 170-210°C steam breaks down most organic soils, while 15-25 bar pressure flushes away physical deposits. For complex-geometry workpieces (blind holes, internal threads, crevices), steam's penetration exceeds that of chemical solvents. Verdict: For standard oil contamination, both are comparable; for heavy oil and complex parts, steam cleaning is superior.

2. Environmental Impact

Chemical cleaning produces solvent-containing wastewater requiring professional treatment before discharge, costing $0.70-$2.00 per ton. Steam cleaning uses only water as the medium — zero chemical additives. Wastewater is simply oil-contaminated condensate that can be discharged after basic oil-water separation. With tightening environmental regulations, steam cleaning's zero-chemical approach helps companies avoid compliance risks. Jieneng steam cleaners use 70-80% less water than traditional washing.

3. Total Cost Comparison

Chemical cleaning has low visible costs (cheap solvents) but high hidden costs: wastewater treatment, PPE, ventilation equipment, and regulatory fines. Steam cleaning has higher equipment cost ($2,000-$7,000) but operating costs are only electricity and water — no solvent purchases or wastewater treatment. At 100,000 parts/year, steam cleaning's 3-year total cost is 30-40% lower than chemical cleaning.

4. Safety

Chemical cleaning involves solvent vapor, fire, and toxicity risks. Operators need PPE, and workshops need ventilation and explosion-proof facilities. Steam cleaning's burn risk is effectively managed through anti-scald piping, automatic pressure relief, and over-temperature cutoff. Jieneng steam cleaners come standard with triple safety protection, offering far better operational safety than chemical cleaning.

5. Versatility

Chemical cleaning is limited by solvent compatibility — certain metals corrode, plastics and rubber may swell. Steam cleaning is compatible with virtually all materials: metals, plastics, rubber, and electronics can all be safely cleaned. Low-pressure mode can even clean precision sensors and circuit boards. Jieneng's adjustable dry/wet steam and pressure settings cover the full range from precision parts to heavy equipment.

6. Regulatory Compliance

GB 38508-2020 (China's cleaning agent VOC content standard) strictly limits VOC content in chemical cleaners, with more traditional solvents being banned or restricted. Steam cleaning produces zero VOC emissions and is unrestricted in any regulatory environment. For companies pursuing Green Factory certification, steam cleaning is the optimal solution for passing cleaner production audits.

Summary

Steam cleaning outperforms chemical cleaning in 4 of 6 dimensions: environmental impact, safety, versatility, and compliance — while matching or exceeding it in cleaning power and total cost.
