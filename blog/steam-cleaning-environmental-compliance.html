Is Steam Cleaning Environmentally Compliant? Zero-Chemical Cleaning Explained

By Guangdong Jieneng 2026-07-16
← Back to Blog

As environmental regulations tighten, traditional chemical cleaning faces elimination. How does steam cleaning achieve zero-chemical cleaning? This article explains the environmental compliance approach in detail.

1. Environmental Principle: Physical Cleaning, Zero Chemicals

Steam cleaning uses the physical energy of high-temperature, high-pressure water vapor — heat dissolves oils, pressure flushes contaminants, steam penetrates crevices. The process uses only pure water as the medium, with zero chemical detergents, solvents, or acids. This means: zero VOC emissions, zero chemical wastewater, zero harmful gas release, zero hazardous waste treatment. Fully compliant with GB 38508-2020 and Water Pollution Prevention Law.

2. Wastewater Treatment

Steam cleaning wastewater consists of removed oil contamination + small amounts of condensate — no chemical solvents. Treatment is simple: oil-water separator physically separates floating oil; separated water can be recycled or discharged after meeting standards; simple filtration removes metal chips and solids. Compare to chemical cleaning wastewater requiring licensed hazardous waste disposal at $0.70-$2.00/ton.

3. Water Savings: 70-80% Less Than Traditional Washing

Traditional high-pressure washing uses 200-500 L/h; steam cleaning uses only 20-50 L/h. Daily comparison:

  • Traditional washing: 1,600-4,000 liters/day
  • Steam cleaning: 160-400 liters/day
  • Daily savings: 1,440-3,600 liters
  • Annual savings: 380-950 tons

2. Carbon Footprint

Steam cleaning's carbon emissions come from electricity. An 18 kW model operating 8 hours/day consumes ~64 kWh, producing ~37 kg CO₂/day (at 0.58 kg CO₂/kWh grid average). Chemical cleaning's emissions include solvent production/transport + wastewater treatment + ventilation — approximately 1.5-2x higher than steam cleaning.

3. Compliance Certifications

Steam cleaning meets: Cleaner Production Audit (zero-chemical approach passes directly), Green Factory Certification (bonus item), EHS Management (no chemical storage risk), Emission Permits (wastewater meets discharge standards), Carbon Neutrality Goals (low emissions support dual-carbon targets). Jieneng equipment is ISO 14001 certified.

4. Industry Compliance Cases

  • Automotive: Replaces solvent cleaners, meets IATF 16949 environmental requirements
  • Electronics: Chemical-free meets RoHS and REACH
  • Food: Chemical-free cleaning passes HACCP
  • Chemical: Explosion-proof steam cleaning meets safety production permits
